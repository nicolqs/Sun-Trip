View of Gournia


Harriet Boyd (Boyd-Hawes following her marriage) arrived on Crete in 1901 and, after hunting for promising sites, surprised the archaeological world by unearthing at Gournia a complete Minoan town.

Phaestos


An Italian, Halbherr came to Crete in 1884 and befriended the Cretan archaeologist Joseph Hadzidakis, with whom he discovered the Bronze Age relics at the Diktian Cave. He later unearthed the palace sites at Phaestos and Agia Triada.

The Grand Staircase


This immense flight of stone steps leading to the propylon (porch) was the main entrance to both the new and the old palaces, and linked the lower and upper levels of the west courtyard.

Fortetza, Chania


Delightful café-bar midway along the mole that encloses the Venetian harbour. This is the best place in town for a sunset drink, with views across the water to the old town.
