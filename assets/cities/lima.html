Places of interest

Alejo Carpentier


Carpentier is known for his cultural journalism focused on Afro-Cuban traditions. He was sent into exile for opposing General Machado. After the Revolution (see 1959: Revolution Triumphs), he headed Cuba’s state publishing house.

Statue of José Martí, Plaza de la Revolución


Perhaps the leading Latin American essayist, poet, and journalist of the 19th century, Martí led the Independence movement (see 1895: War of Independence. He wrote profusely for the cause of social justice, pan-Americanism, and liberty.

La Habichuela


Excellent Yucatecan and Mexican cooking, with Campeche dishes such as cocobichuela (prawns/shrimp, lobster, curry sauce, and rice with coconut). Fine presentation and original touches, all served in the tranquil surroundings of a lush garden (see La Habichuela, Cancún).

Chen Río


With a fine beach all to itself, you can spend a leisurely afternoon here watching the waves and picking over the house specialty, a huge platter of mixed seafood, or sampling the excellent ceviches and salads (see Chen Río, Cozumel).
